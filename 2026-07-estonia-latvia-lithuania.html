The central idea

Why the trip is shaped this way, before the day-by-day. Each choice was made against your profile: fast pace, city-first, local food, light nature only, no driving, value over comfort.

1. Direction: north to south, because price said so

The three capitals sit on a straight line (Vilnius south, Riga middle, Tallinn north), so the only routes that hit all three without doubling back are the two end-to-end directions. Riga is the middle, so flying in or out of Riga would force a backtrack and was ruled out. I priced both clean directions live:

The decisive number is the Vilnius→Munich leg: airBaltic flies it nonstop for €90 in the evening, the cheapest clean leg in the whole search and ~€75 less than the reverse. Monday 3 Aug is the same €90, so there is no price reason to burn an extra night and leave day. We fly home Sunday evening, a full last day in Vilnius.

2. Nights: Tallinn 3 / Riga 2 / Vilnius 3, and why not "biggest city gets most"

Riga is the biggest city by population, but most of that size is residential and Soviet-era outer districts, not visitor ground. Measured in actual sightseeing time, the cities are closer than the population suggests:

CityCity-only timeNightsReason for the night count
Tallinn~1.5 days33rd day is a day trip (seaside), plus a half-day arrival
Riga~1.5-2 days2Compact, concentrated core; done well in 1.5-2 days
Vilnius~2.5 days3Largest old town + Trakai + a full fly-out day

Pure in-city time is almost even between Tallinn and Riga. The night counts differ for reasons unrelated to city size: flight mechanics (half-day Tallinn arrival, full-day Vilnius departure) and day trips (Tallinn's seaside day, Vilnius's Trakai). Riga's tourist core is tight enough that 1.5-2 days covers it without rushing.

3. What I cut, and why

  • Helsinki day trip: out. A 2h ferry each way for a ~6h taste is too much transit, and you have lived in Finland. It earns its own Nordic trip later.
  • Lahemaa National Park: out. No clean public transport, it needs a car or a booked tour. You do not drive, so it is not workable.
  • Jūrmala beach: not needed. The most skippable add-on for a city-first traveller, so Riga stays at 2 nights.

4. Museums: a high bar, not a checklist

  • Tallinn - Seaplane Harbour: in. Rated one of Europe's best, a submarine you walk inside, not a niche stop. It clears the bar.
  • Riga: none anchored. The draw is the architecture itself, seen by walking the Art Nouveau streets. The small Art Nouveau Museum is an optional on-theme stop.
  • Vilnius: none anchored. A streets, churches and Užupis city. The Occupations/KGB museum is left clearly optional.

Tallinn3 nights · Sat 25 - Tue 28 Jul

Theme. A medieval fairy-tale core wrapped in a cool maritime port city. Tallinn pairs the best-preserved old town in northern Europe with a reinvented harbour district of street art and design.

How the days split. Tallinn is small and dense, so I split by character, not by checklist. Day 1 (half day) eases you into the Lower Town. Day 2 pairs the oldest Tallinn (Toompea hill) with the newest (the creative port and its maritime museum). Day 3 is the easy seaside day, deliberately the slow day of the trip.

The route. The walled Old Town is the compact heart and your base. Everything else sits a short tram or bus ride north (the port and Kalamaja) or east (Kadriorg and the coast), so each day fans out from the Old Town and back, no crossing over.

Day 1 · Sat, arrival Day 2 · Sun, Old Town + port Day 3 · Mon, seaside Recommended hotel zone
Where to base: inside or just beside the Old Town (Vanalinn), eastern edge near the Viru Gate / Rotermann Quarter. Everything in the Old Town is on foot, tram 4 runs from the airport to here, and the same line reaches the Bus Station for the Riga coach. One central base, zero daily transit.
Tickets and the Tallinn Card. Nothing in Tallinn needs advance booking. Pay as you go: Seaplane Harbour ~€20-24, St. Olaf's tower ~€5-8, transport ~€2 a ride. Alexander Nevsky cathedral, Kadriorg park and Pirita are free. Your paid items total about €38, while the 72-hour Tallinn Card is around €90, so skip the card and buy individually. The only dress rule in the city: shoulders and knees covered inside the Orthodox cathedral.

Riga2 nights · Tue 28 - Thu 30 Jul

Theme. The grandest and most varied of the three capitals: a medieval Hanseatic Old Town, the densest concentration of Art Nouveau architecture on earth, and one of Europe's biggest markets.

How the days split. Riga splits cleanly in two. The arrival afternoon takes the medieval Old Town, because it is compact and right by the coach terminal where you land. The one full day takes the "New Town" belt: the Art Nouveau streets and the giant Central Market.

The route. Day 2 runs north-to-south in one sweep: start in the elegant Art Nouveau quarter at the top, walk down through the centre and the canal park, and finish at the market and warehouses by the river. You never retrace your steps.

Day 4 · Tue, arrival + Old Town Day 5 · Wed, Art Nouveau + Market Recommended hotel zone
Where to base: in the Old Town (Vecrīga) or just east toward the Quiet Centre. The coach terminal where you arrive, the Old Town, the Central Market, and the foot of the Art Nouveau district are all within a ~15-min walk of each other. One base, no internal transit, and you roll off the coach straight into it.

No strict dress code at Riga's churches.

Vilnius3 nights · Thu 30 Jul - Sun 2 Aug

Theme. The largest and most layered old town in the Baltics: baroque churches by the dozen, an Orthodox and Jewish past, and the bohemian Užupis quarter that declared itself a republic of artists.

How the days split. Day 6 anchors you at the grand northern end (cathedral and castle hill) on arrival. Day 7 walks the full Old Town spine and crosses into Užupis. Day 8 is the Trakai lake-castle day trip. The final morning (Day 9) stays loose before the evening flight.

The route. The Old Town is a long axis running north (cathedral) to south (the city gate). Day 6 takes the north end, Day 7 starts at the south gate and walks up so the two days meet in the middle without overlap, then loops east over the river to Užupis.

Day 6 · Thu, arrival + north anchor Day 7 · Fri, Old Town + Užupis Day 9 · Sun, relaxed + fly out Recommended hotel zone
Where to base: in the Old Town (Senamiestis), south-central, between the Town Hall and the station. You walk in from the bus station, the whole Old Town is on foot, and on the last day the airport train is an 8-minute hop from the same station. Walk-in, walk-out, no transit in between.
Tickets and the Vilnius Pass. Almost everything here is free to walk, and your paid sights are few and cheap. Gediminas Tower €8 (funicular up €1 each way, or walk the path free). St. John's bell tower, the tallest viewpoint in the Old Town, €3. The university courtyards about €2. The cathedral itself is free; its separate bell tower is €5 and skippable, since you already climb Gediminas and St. John's. Optional on the last day, the MO Museum is about €10. That totals roughly €11-23, so the Vilnius Pass (more than that for 24 hours, and it does not cover Trakai) does not pay off for your plan. Nothing here needs advance booking. Dress rule: shoulders and knees covered inside the cathedral, the Gates of Dawn chapel and St. Anne's.

Trakai day tripDay 8 · Sat 1 Aug

Why Trakai is worth a whole day: it is the only island castle in this part of Europe, a red Gothic castle on an island in a lake reached by footbridges, the postcard of Lithuania. It is gentle (flat lakeside walking, an optional rowboat), it is a different landscape after three city cores, and it carries a real food-and-culture hook: the Karaim, a tiny Turkic community settled here in the 1300s, whose wooden houses line the main street and whose pastry, kibinai, is the thing to eat by the water. You get the castle, the lake and a food you cannot get elsewhere, and you can skip the interior museum entirely and still get the whole point.
Today: a there-and-back day trip from your Vilnius base, so it does not touch the no-backtrack main route. You take a short train out, eat kibinai by the lake, cross the footbridges to the island castle, walk the shore, and head back for a relaxed final Vilnius evening. Placed Saturday, not the Sunday fly-out day, so it is never rushed against a flight. Highlight: the island castle floating on the lake.
